I am a Ph.D. student in Statistics at the University of Auckland, where I also graduated with B.Sc.(Honours) and M.Sc. (First Class), all majoring in statistics. My research interests are survival analysis, longitudinal data and sampling.

My Ph.D. research is about joint modelling of longitudinal medical cost data and survival in complex samples. The aim of the research is to combine survey calibration with standard joint models, by using calibrated sampling weights in the joint likelihood.
